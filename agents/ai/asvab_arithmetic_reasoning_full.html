Arithmetic Reasoning - Full Comprehensive Guide

Subtopic 1.1: Fractions & Decimals

Fractions represent parts of a whole. A Mixed Number combines a whole integer and a fraction (e.g., 1 3/5). An Improper Fraction has a numerator greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 8/5). The Reciprocal is the inverted form of a fraction (e.g., reciprocal of 3/7 is 7/3). To divide fractions, multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction. Decimals use place values like Tenths (0.1), Hundredths (0.01), and Thousandths (0.001). To multiply decimals, multiply normally and count the total decimal places for the result.

Subtopic 1.4: Math Operations & Properties

Number types include Whole Numbers (0 to infinity), Integers (positive/negative whole numbers), Rational Numbers (ratios of integers), and Real Numbers (all rational/irrational). Basic properties: Commutative (a + b = b + a), Associative ((a + b) + c = a + (b + c)), Distributive (a(b + c) = ab + ac), and Identity (0 for addition, 1 for multiplication). Even/Odd rules: Even + Even = Even, Odd + Odd = Even, Odd + Even = Odd. Negative numbers: Negative x Negative = Positive, Negative x Positive = Negative.

Subtopic 1.9: Number Properties (Prime, Factors, Multiples)

Absolute Value is the distance from zero on a number line (|x|). Prime numbers are divisible only by 1 and themselves (2, 3, 5, 7...). Composite numbers have more than two factors. Greatest Common Factor (GCF) is the largest factor shared by two numbers. Least Common Multiple (LCM) is the smallest multiple shared by two numbers. Divisibility rules: 3 (sum of digits divisible by 3), 4 (last two digits divisible by 4), 6 (divisible by 2 and 3), 9 (sum of digits divisible by 9).

Subtopic 1.3: Distance, Rate & Time

The standard formula is Distance = Rate x Time (d = r * t). Average speed is the total distance divided by the total time. If a car travels 120 miles in 2 hours, the rate is 60 mph. Buzzwords for word problems include "difference" for subtraction and "product" for multiplication.
