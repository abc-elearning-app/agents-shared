Subtopic 7.1: Machines & Mechanical Advantage

Mechanical Advantage (MA) is the amplification of force: MA = Load / Effort or Effort Distance / Load Distance. For Gears, MA is the ratio of driven teeth to driving teeth. Inclined Planes have MA = Length / Rise. Levers: Class 1 (Fulcrum in middle, e.g., seesaw), Class 2 (Load in middle, e.g., wheelbarrow), Class 3 (Effort in middle). Pulleys: MA is the number of ropes attached to the load. Screws and Wedges also provide MA by trading distance for force. Wheel and Axle MA is the ratio of wheel radius to axle radius.

Subtopic 7.2: Fundamentals of Mechanics

Newton's Laws: 1. Inertia (object resists change), 2. F = ma (Force = Mass x Acceleration), 3. Action/Reaction. Work = Force x Distance (Joules). Power = Work / Time (Watts). Energy Types: Kinetic (motion, 1/2 mv^2) and Potential (position, mgh). Fluid Dynamics: Air/Water pressure increases with depth. Hydraulic Lifts use Pascal's Principle (Pressure is transmitted equally): A1 * D1 = A2 * D2. Material Properties: Conduction (heat transfer), Flexibility (bending), Malleability (reforming).
