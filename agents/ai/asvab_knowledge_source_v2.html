1. ARITHMETIC REASONING (AR)

1.1. Fractions

Fractions represent a part of a whole. In the ASVAB, you must be able to perform all four operations and simplify them. Simplifying: Divide both the numerator and denominator by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF). Example: 12/18 -> GCF is 6 -> 2/3. Addition/Subtraction: Requires a Common Denominator. Find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of the denominators. Example: 1/4 + 1/6 -> LCM is 12 -> 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12. Multiplication: Multiply straight across (numerator * numerator, denominator * denominator). Example: 2/3 * 4/5 = 8/15. Division: Use the "Keep-Change-Flip" rule (multiply by the reciprocal). Example: 1/2 / 3/4 -> 1/2 * 4/3 = 4/6 = 2/3.

1.2. Statistics

Mean (Average): Sum of all terms divided by the number of terms. Formula: Sum / Count = Average. Median: The middle value when numbers are sorted in order. If there are two middle numbers, average them. Mode: The number that appears most frequently in a set. Range: The difference between the highest and lowest values. Example: Set {2, 5, 5, 8, 10} -> Mean: 6, Median: 5, Mode: 5, Range: 8.

1.3. Distance, Rate & Time

The Magic Formula: D = R * T (Distance = Rate * Time). To find Rate: R = D / T. To find Time: T = D / R. Key Tip: Ensure units match (e.g., if the rate is in miles per hour, time must be in hours). Example: A car travels at 60 mph for 2.5 hours. Distance = 60 * 2.5 = 150 miles.

1.5. Percentage

Percentages are "parts per hundred." The Percent Formula: Part = Percent * Whole (or Is / Of = % / 100). Percent Increase/Decrease: (New Value - Old Value) / Old Value * 100. Simple Interest: I = P * r * t (Interest = Principal * Rate * Time). Example: A $200 item is 15% off. Discount = 200 * 0.15 = $30. Sale price = $170.

1.7. Geometry

Rectangle: P = 2l + 2w; A = l * w. Triangle: A = 1/2 * base * height. Circle: C = 2*pi*r (Circumference); A = pi*r^2 (Area). Pythagorean Theorem: a^2 + b^2 = c^2 (Used for right triangles to find a missing side). Common Triplets: 3-4-5, 5-12-13. Volume (Box): V = l * w * h.

7. MECHANICAL COMPREHENSION (MC)

Simple Machines

Levers: A rigid bar rotating around a fulcrum. Class 1: Fulcrum in the middle (seesaw, scissors, crowbar). Class 2: Load in the middle (wheelbarrow, nutcracker). Class 3: Effort in the middle (tweezers, broom, human arm). Pulleys: Fixed Pulley changes direction (MA = 1). Movable Pulley reduces effort (MA = 2). Block and Tackle: System of pulleys that increases MA significantly. Wheel and Axle: Large wheel connected to small axle (doorknob, steering wheel). Inclined Plane: Slanted surface to lift heavy loads with less force over distance (ramp). MA = Output Force / Input Force or MA = Effort Distance / Resistance Distance.

Physics Fundamentals

Newton's Laws: 1. Inertia (object at rest stays at rest), 2. F = ma (Force = mass * acceleration), 3. Action/Reaction (every action has equal/opposite reaction). Work (W) = Force * Distance (Joules). Power (P) = Work / Time (Watts). Friction: Resistance encountered when moving over surface.

4. ELECTRONICS INFORMATION (EI)

Ohm's Law

Relationship between voltage, current, and resistance: V = I * R. V (Voltage): Electrical potential (Volts). I (Current): Flow of charge (Amperes/Amps). R (Resistance): Opposition to flow (Ohms). Circuits: Series Circuit: Components in single loop; Rt = R1 + R2... Parallel Circuit: Components in multiple branches; 1/Rt = 1/R1 + 1/R2... Power: P = V * I (Watts). Components: Resistor: Limits current. Capacitor: Stores energy in electric field. Inductor: Stores energy in magnetic field. Diode: One-way valve. Transistor: Switch or amplifier.

3. AUTO & SHOP INFORMATION (AS)

Four-Stroke Cycle

1. Intake: Intake valve opens, piston moves down, draws air-fuel mixture. 2. Compression: Both valves close, piston moves up, compresses mixture. 3. Power (Combustion): Spark plug ignites mixture, forces piston down. 4. Exhaust: Exhaust valve opens, piston moves up, expels waste gases. Components: Crankshaft: linear to rotational motion. Camshaft: controls valve timing. Radiator: cooling system heat dissipation. Carburetor/Fuel Injector: mixes fuel/air.

Shop Tools

Wrenches: Open-end (light duty), Box-end (better grip), Socket (fast), Torque (specific pressure). Pliers: Needle-nose (small spaces), Slip-joint (adjustable), Vise-Grips (locking). Measuring: Micrometers and Calipers (high precision).
