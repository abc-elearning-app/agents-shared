Electronics Information - Full Technical Guide

Subtopic 4.1: Electricity & Atomic Structure

Electron Flow Theory states that negatively charged electrons flow from the negative terminal (anode) to the positive terminal (cathode). Atoms consist of Protons (positive), Neutrons (no charge), and Electrons (negative). The Valence Shell is the outermost orbital; its electrons are the most reactive and determine conductivity. Conductors (like copper) allow easy flow, Insulators (like rubber) resist flow, and Semiconductors (like graphite) have middle conductivity that increases with heat.

Subtopic 4.2: Electrical Systems & Circuits

Ohm’s Law: I = V / R (Current = Voltage / Resistance). Voltage is electric potential, Current is the rate of flow (Amps), and Resistance is measured in Ohms. In a Series Circuit, components follow one path and current is the same throughout. In a Parallel Circuit, there are multiple paths and voltage is same across branches. Power formula: P = V * I (Watts). AC (Alternating Current) reverses direction periodically, while DC (Direct Current) flows in one direction. Safety devices like Fuses and Circuit Breakers prevent overloads. Capacitors store energy (Farads), Diodes allow one-way flow (Rectification), and Transistors amplify signals using Base, Collector, and Emitter terminals.
